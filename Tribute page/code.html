Srinivasa Ramanujan

The Man Who Knew Infinity

Here's a time line of Srinivasa Ramanujan

  • December 22, 1887:- Srinivasa Ramanujan was born in Erode, Madras Presidency, British India (now Tamil Nadu, India).
  • 1892:-Ramanujan began attending primary school in Kumbakonam, but his academic performance was poor due to his fascination with mathematics and disinterest in other subjects.
  • 1900:-Ramanujan dropped out of school due to health issues and began to study mathematics independently.
  • 1903:-Ramanujan discovered theorems in trigonometry and hypergeometric series, and independently rediscovered Euler's identity.
  • 1909:- Ramanujan married Janaki Ammal, a girl from his community who was ten years old at the time.
  • 1911:-Ramanujan wrote to G.H. Hardy, a prominent mathematician at the University of Cambridge, seeking guidance and mentorship.
  • 1913:- Ramanujan moved to Cambridge, England, with financial support from Hardy and the Indian government.
  • 1914-1918:-World War I disrupted Ramanujan's work, and he struggled with illness and poverty during this period.
  • 1918:- Ramanujan was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society, becoming the first Indian to be so honored.
  • 1919:-Ramanujan was awarded a Bachelor of Science degree by Cambridge University.
  • 1920:-Ramanujan returned to India due to declining health, and he died on April 26 of that year at the age of 32.

Here's a time line of Srinivasa Ramanujan Awards

  • 1911:-Ramanujan was awarded the K. Ranganatha Rao Prize for Mathematics by the University of Madras.
  • 1913:- He was awarded the first-class scholarship in Mathematics by the Government Arts College in Kumbakonam.
  • 1917Ramanujan was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of London, becoming one of the youngest Fellows in the society's history.
  • 1918:-He was awarded the Fellow of the Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1919:-Ramanujan was elected a Fellow of the Cambridge Philosophical Society
  • 1920:-He was awarded the Senior Wrangler title, which is the highest distinction in Mathematics at the University of Cambridge.
  • 1921:-Ramanujan was elected a Fellow of the Indian Academy of Sciences.
  • 1922:-He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh.
  • 1923:-Ramanujan was awarded the Padma Bhushan, one of the highest civilian awards in India.
  • 1924:-He was elected a Fellow of the London Mathematical Society.
  • 1926:- Ramanujan was awarded the Fellow of the University of Madras.
  • 2012:-The Indian government declared December 22 as National Mathematics Day, in honor of Ramanujan's birth anniversary.

One of the famous quotes by Srinivasa Ramanujan is:-

"An equation means nothing to me unless it expresses a thought of God."

This quote highlights Ramanujan's deep spiritual and philosophical beliefs and how they influenced his mathematical work. He believed that mathematics was not just a human invention, but rather a divine language that could reveal profound truths about the universe
